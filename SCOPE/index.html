SCOPE: Spatial-Channel Optimization with Efficient Adaptive Fusion for Real-Time Object Detection in Autonomous Vehicles

Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering · Concordia University

Overview

Abstract

Reliable object detection is a critical component of perception systems in autonomous driving, where recognizing objects and understanding complex scenes are essential for safety and decision-making. Among existing detectors, EfficientDet offers a promising trade-off between accuracy and efficiency through its compound scaling and Bi-directional Feature Pyramid Network (BiFPN). However, its performance is hindered by three key challenges: (1) loss of fine-grained spatial details due to backbone downsampling, (2) limited contextual awareness resulting from suboptimal feature fusion in BiFPN layers, and (3) increased inference latency caused by stacking multiple BiFPN modules. Unlike conventional BiFPN architectures that rely on static multi-scale fusion, autonomous driving scenes require adaptive feature selection mechanisms capable of handling severe scale variation and real-time latency constraints. To overcome these limitations, we propose SCOPE, a latency-aware adaptive fusion framework designed for real-time autonomous driving perception. SCOPE enhances the EfficientNet backbone through spatial and channel attention refinement within the MBConv blocks, while adaptive channel-wise feature weighting is utilized in a novel efficient attention-guided feature fusion strategy to improve multi-scale representation quality and fusion efficiency. The proposed single-layer adaptive attention fusion method dynamically prioritizes informative multi-scale features while reducing redundant feature propagation and computational overhead. Extensive experiments on multiple autonomous driving datasets demonstrate that SCOPE improves detection accuracy, while simultaneously reducing computational complexity and inference latency, thereby outperforming state-of-the-art object detection methods in terms of both detection performance and computational efficiency.

Method

  • Spatial-channel feature refinement. SCOPE inserts CBAM into selected EfficientNet MBConv blocks so that channel attention first emphasizes informative feature responses and spatial attention then focuses the representation on relevant object regions. This refinement is designed to preserve discriminative details that can otherwise be weakened by repeated downsampling.
  • Adaptive attention-guided feature fusion. Instead of relying on static scalar fusion weights, the proposed Adaptive Attention BiFPN applies channel-wise gating to multi-scale feature maps. Global context is compressed through pooling and fully connected transformations, producing scale-aware channel weights that dynamically prioritize useful features before fusion.
  • Single-layer latency-aware BiFPN. While EfficientDet increases BiFPN depth as the model scale grows, SCOPE fixes the fusion depth to one layer across all model variants. The adaptive gating mechanism compensates for the reduced depth, limiting redundant feature propagation and decreasing FLOPs and inference latency.
  • Efficient prediction heads. The fused pyramid features are shared by lightweight classification and box-regression subnetworks composed of depthwise separable convolutions, enabling accurate multi-scale prediction without introducing a large computational burden.
  • Compound model scaling. SCOPE follows the EfficientDet scaling philosophy for image resolution, feature width and prediction-head depth, while retaining the proposed attention-enhanced backbone and fixed-depth adaptive fusion design from S0 to S7.

Results

Across the compound model scales, SCOPE provides its largest accuracy gains in lightweight configurations and increasingly strong efficiency gains at larger scales. On Udacity, SCOPE-S0 improves overall mAP from 52.32% to 54.41% while reducing FLOPs from 2.55B to 1.91B. On KITTI, SCOPE-S3 reaches 67.28% mAP compared with 65.41% for EfficientDet-D3. The latency advantage grows with scale because SCOPE retains a single adaptive fusion layer rather than stacking progressively deeper BiFPN modules. The in-domain and cross-domain evaluations on BDD100K and nuScenes further show that the proposed spatial-channel refinement and adaptive fusion remain effective under changes in scene distribution.

Datasets

  • Udacity Self-Driving Car Dataset: primary training and evaluation benchmark for compound scaling and comparison with state-of-the-art detectors.
  • KITTI: validation benchmark used to assess the proposed architecture across scales and evaluate precision-recall behavior.
  • BDD100K: used for both in-domain experiments and cross-domain evaluation of models trained exclusively on Udacity.
  • nuScenes: used to assess generalization to a different autonomous-driving data distribution without target-domain fine-tuning.
